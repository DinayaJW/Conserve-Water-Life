Sea Creatures: Diversity, Importance, and Conservation


Sea creatures, including fish, mammals, invertebrates, and plants, inhabit oceans and saltwater bodies. They are crucial to marine ecosystems and range from microscopic plankton to the largest animal, the blue whale.


The decreasing existence of various marine animals is a growing environmental issue that needs dire attention. This not only leads to the extinction of many unique species of sea animals but also the welfare of living beings underwater and on land alike. Many actions are currently taking place of marine life, mainly to prohibit any form of human activity, such as fishing or oil development so as to avoid overfishing and pollution of the ocean, with the of conserving marine habitat.


Moreover, taking into consideration the harmful repercussions of plastic pollution at sea, which could lead sea animals, namely turtles and seals to be entangled and suffocated or badly injured, proper disposal of plastics and usage of non-plastic elements whenever possible is a main method that is implemented for this cause.
